## Adaptation Rules

### Format Conversion
- Use standard INT./EXT. scene headers
- Shot types: WIDE SHOT / MEDIUM SHOT / CLOSE-UP / EXTREME CLOSE-UP
- Time of day: DAY / NIGHT / DAWN / DUSK
- Character names in ALL CAPS
- First appearance includes age and brief description in parentheses

### Cultural Adaptation
- Royal court/dynasty themes -> High society / Corporate dynasty
- Class-based conflicts -> Social mobility / Old money vs new money
- Traditional customs -> Localized equivalents (charity gala instead of court banquet)
- Supernatural elements -> Adapt to target audience familiarity (werewolf/vampire for Western audiences)
- Legal disputes -> Appropriate legal framework for target market
- Preserve cultural uniqueness with brief contextual explanation where needed

### Dialogue Adaptation
- No literal translation — use natural, idiomatic English
- Maintain the rhythm and impact of dialogue
- Localize humor, slang, and cultural references
- Preserve the satisfaction factor while adjusting cultural context
- Match the dialogue style to the target platform audience

### Platform Optimization
- ReelShort: Romance-focused, moderate suggestive content OK, Billionaire/Werewolf most popular
- DramaBox: Broader genres, period/fantasy well-received
- General: Violence within TV-14, romance PG-13 to TV-MA range

### Proven International Hit Elements
- Billionaire + Forced Marriage
- Werewolf / Alpha Mate
- Secret Baby + Reunion
- Revenge + Hidden Identity
- Mafia Romance
- Vampire / Supernatural Romance

【Shot Scale】
- Extreme wide: epic scale, tiny figures
- Wide/full: establish environment, character relationships
- Medium: dialogue, interaction, daily scenes
- Close-up: emotion, reaction
- Extreme close-up: eyes, lips, a teardrop, key props

【Camera Angles】
- Eye-level: natural, daily
- Low-angle: power, intimidation (action/epic)
- High-angle: vulnerability, overview (epic scenes)
- Over-the-shoulder: dialogue, confrontation
- Dutch angle: unease, tension (suspense)
- POV: immersion

【Camera Movement】
- Static: gaze, silence (ONLY for close-ups of props/details)
- Slow push/pull: emotional buildup, reveal
- Follow: character movement
- Snap zoom in/out: shock, impact
- Orbit/crane up/dive: ritual, epic grandeur
- Handheld shake: chaos, tension (action)
- Steadicam/gimbal tracking: smooth long-take chase, one-take sequences
- Aerial rise/dive/follow: drone narrative shots, world-building or chase overview

【Advanced Visual Techniques】(apply when visual cue brackets are detected)

Speed Ramping:
- For: fight/chase/key strike moments
- Normal speed → extreme slow-mo for impact → accelerate to finish
- video_prompt example: "young man throws a right hook, fist approaching jaw in sudden slow motion with visible sweat droplets, then rapid acceleration as opponent recoils, handheld low-angle tracking"

Bullet Time:
- For: critical strikes, explosions, falling moments
- Time freeze + 360° orbit slow rotation, highlight details and impact
- video_prompt example: "fist about to connect with face — time freezes, camera orbits 360° in ultra-slow rotation, debris and hair suspended mid-air, light cutting through dust"

Montage / Cross-cutting:
- For: flashbacks, emotional climax, time passage, contrast
- Memory scenes: warm tone + soft focus; reality: cold tone + sharp focus, rapid alternation
- video_prompt must note tone shifts: "frame shifts to warm golden soft-focus" / "cuts back to cold blue sharp reality"

Aerial Narrative:
- For: world-building, battlefield overview, chase from above, grand transitions
- Extreme high altitude overview → dynamic follow → cut to character close-up (God's-eye → subjective)
- video_prompt example: "drone extreme high shot overlooking the city, camera rapidly dives through streets, finally locks onto young man running with bloodied face, screaming skyward"

One-Take (Long Shot):
- For: chase sequences, extended fight, continuous emotional scenes
- Steadicam/gimbal long take, no cuts, enhances realism and immersion
- video_prompt example: "steadicam follows young man sprinting through corridor, bursting through door, spinning to face knife-wielding middle-aged man, both grapple and crash into vase — single unbroken shot"

Atmosphere Building:
- Suspense/thriller: low-key lighting + high contrast + rim light silhouette + wet ground reflections + fog
- Rainy night: streetlamp/neon reflected on wet pavement, rain droplet close-ups + dark-tone facial lighting
- video_prompt example: "rainy night street, neon red-blue glow reflected on wet pavement, young man approaches backlit showing only dark silhouette, rain splashing off shoulders into mist, low-angle slow push-in"

⚠️ 【Motion-First Principle — Core Rule】

Every video_prompt MUST contain movement:

1. Character action vocabulary (MUST use):

   - Hands: raises hand, waves, points, clenches fist, sets down, picks up, touches
   - Body: walks, turns around, stands up, sits down, leans forward, steps back, approaches

2. Camera movement vocabulary (prefer these over "static"):
   - Gentle: slow push-in, gentle follow, slight sway, slow orbit

4. Even dialogue scenes must have motion:

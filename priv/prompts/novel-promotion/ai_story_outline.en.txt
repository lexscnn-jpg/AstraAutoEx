## Core Creative Rules

- Dense satisfaction points: face-slapping, comebacks, misunderstandings, emotional peaks — at least 2-3 emotional highs per episode
- Dialogue style: colloquial, witty, trending memes and pop culture references, slightly edgy, punchy and rhythmic — sounds like real people talking
- Episode pacing: 30-second opening hook -> escalating conflict + twist -> big cliffhanger ending
- Overall structure: three-act (setup -> confrontation -> resolution), with a major twist at 1/3, the biggest crisis at 2/3, and an emotional climax at the end

## Character System Requirements

Target 20-30 named characters total, layered as follows:
- Core protagonists: 3-5 (hero/heroine, main antagonist) — must have complete character arcs and emotional transformations
- Key supporting: 6-10 (allies, rivals, love interests, mentors, family elders) — each with independent subplots and clear motivations
- Minor characters: several (subordinates, colleagues, etc.) — used to drive specific plot points
- Every character must have a clear desire, secret, or inner conflict — no one-dimensional tools

## Episode Count & Duration — Hard Requirements

- Total episodes: 65-80 (the market-proven sweet spot for viral hits); for exceptionally complex stories, up to 100 max
- Per-episode density: 2-3 specific scenes/events, enough to fill 90-180 seconds of video (approximately 6-15 storyboard shots)
- Zero filler: every episode must advance the plot or reveal something new about a character — no pure transition/flashback/filler episodes
